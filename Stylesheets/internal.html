About Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam

  1. Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu,according to Wikipedia.
  2. He had a humble upbringing and sold newspapers as a child to support his family.
  3. He pursued his passion for science and engineering, graduating in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and specializing in Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  4. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, but his legacy continues to inspire generations.
  5. Kalam began his career at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  6. He was a key figure in the 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests, solidifying India as a nuclear power.
  7. In 2002, he was elected as the 11th President of India and served a five-year term
  8. As president, he was affectionately known as the "People's President" for his accessibility and dedication to connecting with ordinary citizens, especially the youth.
  9. After his presidency, Kalam remained active as an author, educator, and motivational speaker, inspiring countless students and young people to pursue their dreams and contribute to nation-building.
